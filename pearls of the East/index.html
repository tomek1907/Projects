Hongkong

Hong Kong was established as a colony of the British Empire after the Qing Empire ceded Hong Kong Island from Xin'an County at the end of the First Opium War in 1841 then again in 1842. The colony expanded to the Kowloon Peninsula in 1860 after the Second Opium War and was further extended when Britain obtained a 99-year lease of the New Territories in 1898. British Hong Kong was occupied by Imperial Japan from 1941 to 1945 during World War II; British administration resumed after the surrender of Japan. The whole territory was transferred to China in 1997. As one of China's two special administrative regions (the other being Macau), Hong Kong maintains separate governing and economic systems from that of mainland China under the principle of "one country, two systems".

Top Places

Lantau Island The largest island in Hong Kong located at the mouth of the Pearl River. The island is located in the Islands district, except for the northern part, which belongs to the Tsuen Wan district. The area of ​​the island is 147.16 km².
Victoria Peak From there you can admire the view of the port and the city from the Sky Terrace 428 viewing platform (428 m above sea level) and do some shopping in one of the shops and eat in a restaurant or bar located inside one of the architectural icons, the Peak Tower.
The Avenue of Stars It is a magical promenade above Tsim Sha Tsui. There are monuments and plaques commemorating movie stars and people who made magic a reality. One of its most important points is the over two-meter statue of the kung-fu legend, Bruce Lee.

Macau

One of the two special administrative regions of the People's Republic of China (the other being Hong Kong), located on the east coast of China. Macau lies on the western side of the Pearl River Estuary, bordering Guangdong Province to the north, and the South China Sea to the south and east. The economy is based mainly on tourism and gambling, but some industries are also developing dynamically, including clothing, electronic and toy industries. Macau is one of the richest regions in the world.

Taiwan

Until 1971, the Republic of China was one of the founding members of the United Nations. The rapprochement of People's China and the USA in the early 1970s and the pressure of the PRC authorities meant that the Republic of China is now recognized by only 14 countries (including the Vatican, Paraguay and economically underdeveloped island countries of the Pacific region, countries of Central America and the Caribbean Sea). In 1999–2001, it was also recognized by Macedonia in exchange for financial assistance (the so-called dollar diplomacy). Since January 1, 2002, the Republic of China has been a member of the WTO under the name of the Separate Customs Area of ​​Taiwan, Penghu, Kinmen and Matsu.

Top Places

Taipei 101 The building, designed by C.Y.Lee & Partners and constructed for the most part by KTRT Joint Venture and Samsung Engineering & Construction. Until January 4, 2010, it was officially awarded the title of the tallest completed building in the world.
Night Market Night market is the famous market in Taipei, were you can try regional food, buy many interesting things and feel the magic vibe of the island.
Mausoleum A building in the center of Taipei, in the Zhongzheng district, erected in memory of the President of the Republic of China Chiang Kai-shek. The Hall of Remembrance is located in the eastern part of the complex with an area of ​​approx. 250,000 sq. m².
